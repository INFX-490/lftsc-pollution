FAQ Section

Q: What is air pollution?
A: Air pollution is the presence of harmful or toxic chemicals and compounds in the air that are at levels that can be detrimental to one's health.

Q: What kinds of air pollution exist?
A: The most common air pollutants include nitrogen dioxide (NO2), ozone (O3), sulfur dioxide (SO2), particulate matter (PM), carbon monoxide (CO), and lead (Pb).

Q: What are the causes of air pollution?
A: Human activity is the leading cause of air pollution. These activities include transportaion, industrial work, mining, etc. Secondary causes include natural phenomenons such as volcanic eruptions and wild fires though these are rarer and cause significantly less harm to the environment than what humans contribute on a daily basis.

Q: What effects on human health does air pollution have?
A: As the level of exposure and toxicity of pollutants vary, so do the potential harmful effects on humans. These effects can be as simple as coughing and skin irritation, but can also lead to more severe diseases such as cancer.

Q: How can air pollution be mitigated?
A: Some methods include:
- Conserving energy through energy efficient light bulbs and appliances.
- Minimizing gas usage through public transportation, carpools, biking, or walking.
- Utilizing environmentally friendly cleaners, paints, and other chemicals.
